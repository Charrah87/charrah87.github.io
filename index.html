About Me

Built from the system up.

My career started inside a production environment. I joined Zip Realty shortly after the launch of the Zap platform, a web application used by thousands of agents across national real estate brands. My role was to investigate issues reported by users, reproduce bugs, and work with engineers to diagnose root causes across APIs, databases, and application code.

As I gained experience, my role expanded beyond troubleshooting. I began identifying patterns across issues, documenting system behaviors, and working with product and engineering teams to design solutions that would prevent problems from happening again. Over time I became the bridge between support, engineering, and product — helping translate real user problems into improvements to the platform.

The most important question in any system issue is simple: what problem are we actually trying to solve?

That perspective shaped how I approach product management today. I focus on understanding systems deeply — how applications, APIs, and data pipelines interact — and making decisions that improve reliability, scalability, and user experience at the same time.
