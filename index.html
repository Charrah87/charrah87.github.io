About Me

Built from the system up.

Most recently I was a Senior PM at Anywhere Real Estate, where I owned the roadmap, delivery, and technical decisions for an enterprise API platform serving five national real estate brands at 20M+ requests per day. I managed one team with two tech leads running ETL and API development sub-teams. For platform-wide initiatives that crossed org lines, I led teams I didn't own to get the work done.

The job runs the full loop. Research and discovery first: understanding what the platform needs from the engineers building it, the support data coming in, and the brands depending on it. That becomes a feature request, then a spec, then a design, then a sprint, then a test cycle, then a release. And somewhere in that chain there's always a roadblock. Feed specs that don't match reality. Vendor timelines that slip. Engineering bandwidth that gets pulled mid-sprint. I plan for all of it and still ship.

Every action starts with the question: what is the problem we are trying to solve?

I spent years inside the production environment before I ever owned a roadmap. That's where I learned how APIs fail, why pipelines break, and what it takes to build something that holds in production. By the time I was managing product, I already knew what engineers were dealing with. And they knew I knew. That background is what keeps the PM work grounded.

AI In My Workflow

Using AI to move faster on real work.

I use AI the same way I use any engineering tool: it's only as useful as the technical foundation behind it.

Since mid-2025 I've been using GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT, and Claude as regular parts of my workflow. Copilot for reviewing API mock changes in our QA environment before production approval. ChatGPT and Claude for analyzing large data sets, drafting PRDs, and handling the writing that used to eat hours: emails, documentation, stakeholder summaries.

Because I work in production systems, I don't treat AI output as final. I treat it as a fast first draft that I validate against real system context. Speed plus accuracy. That's what I'm always after.
